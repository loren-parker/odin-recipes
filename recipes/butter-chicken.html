Butter Chicken

A delicious and easy indian dish

The perfect sauce to accompany your chicken; it can be as mild or as spicy as you want by adjusting the cayenne. I like to serve it with naan bread.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp peanut oil (divided)
  • 1 shallot (finely chopped)
  • 1/4 white onion (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp garam masala (divided)
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1 cup half-and-half cream
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 pinch salt and black pepper
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup water

Steps

  1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Saute shallot and onion until translucent.
  2. Stir in butter, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, 1 tsp garam masala, chili powder, cumin, and bay leaf. Cook while stirring for one minute. Add tomato sauce and cook for two minutes, stirring frequently.
  3. Stir in cream and yogurt. Reduce heat to low and simmer for ten minutes, stirring frequently. Add salt and pepper. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Heat remaining oil in a large skillet over medium heat. cook chicken until lightly browned; roughly 10 minutes.
  5. Reduce heat and season with remaining garam masala and cayenne. Stir in a few spoonfuls of sauce and simmer until liquid has reduced and chicken is no longer pink. Add cooked chicken into sauce and stir together.
  6. Dissolve cornstarch into water then mix into sauce. Cook for five to ten minutes, or until thickened.
  7. Serve over rice with naan.
